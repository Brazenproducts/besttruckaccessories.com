Truck Accessories Buyer's Guide

Not sure where to start? Here's what actually matters when choosing truck accessories — no marketing fluff.

🪑 Seat Covers — What to Look For

The single most important factor is fit. Universal seat covers bunch, slide, and look terrible within a month. Always buy vehicle-specific covers.

  • Material matters: Carhartt duck weave and heavy canvas are the gold standard for truck durability. Neoprene is great for water resistance. Leatherette looks nice but cracks over time.
  • Airbag compatibility: Make sure covers have sewn-in airbag seams. Cheap universal covers can block side airbags — that's a safety issue, not just a fit issue.
  • Installation: Good covers take 30-60 minutes to install properly. If it takes 5 minutes, it's going to look like it took 5 minutes.
  • Our pick: Covercraft Carhartt Covers — tough, vehicle-specific, made in USA.

📦 Tonneau Covers — Hard vs Soft vs Retractable

Your choice depends on three things: security needs, budget, and how often you need full bed access.

  • Soft roll-up: Cheapest, lightest, full bed access. But zero security and can flap at speed.
  • Hard tri-fold: Best balance of security, weather protection, and access. Our recommended starting point.
  • Retractable: Slickest operation, locks anywhere, but expensive and the canister eats bed space.
  • Key spec: Check the weight rating. If you want to put cargo ON TOP of the cover, you need a hard folder rated for it.
  • Our pick: BAKFlip MX4 — hard folding, 500lb on-top rating, decade-long durability.

🧹 Floor Mats — Custom Fit is Non-Negotiable

Universal floor mats are a waste of money. They slide, they leave gaps, and they don't contain anything. Spend the extra $50-100 on laser-measured custom fit.

  • Material: TPE rubber is odorless and flexible. Traditional rubber is cheaper but can smell.
  • Wall height: Higher walls = more containment. Look for at least 1" of wall around the perimeter.
  • Retention: Make sure they hook into your truck's factory retention posts. Sliding mats are dangerous.
  • Our pick: WeatherTech FloorLiner HP — the original and still the best.

📹 Dash Cams — Resolution Isn't Everything

Everyone focuses on 4K vs 1080p, but night performance and reliability matter more. A 4K cam with bad night vision won't capture the plate that hit you at 10pm.

  • Sensor: Sony STARVIS or STARVIS 2 sensors dominate low-light. Check the sensor, not just the resolution.
  • Parking mode: If your truck sits in lots, buffered parking mode catches hit-and-runs. Requires hardwire kit.
  • Heat tolerance: Trucks get HOT inside. Capacitor-based cams (not battery) survive better in extreme heat.
  • Storage: Use high-endurance microSD cards. Regular cards die fast under constant write cycles.
  • Our pick: Viofo A229 Pro Duo — 4K front, 2K rear, STARVIS 2, excellent parking mode.

📱 Phone Mounts — Vibration is the Enemy

Trucks vibrate more than cars. That vibration kills cheap mounts and can even damage your phone's camera OIS system over time.

  • Mount type: Mechanical locks (RAM, Quad Lock) beat magnetic mounts on rough roads.
  • Base: Suction cups fail in heat. Clamp or bolt mounts are more reliable for trucks.
  • Camera warning: Apple has warned that motorcycle vibrations can damage iPhone cameras. Trucks are less severe, but a dampened mount is still smart.
  • Our pick: RAM X-Grip — military-grade, universal, indestructible.

🪢 Recovery Gear — Don't Cheap Out on Safety

Recovery gear is safety equipment. A failed strap or shackle at tension can kill. Buy rated gear from reputable brands and inspect it before every use.

  • Kinetic vs static: Kinetic ropes stretch and store energy for smooth pulls. Static straps are for winching only — never snatch with a static strap.
  • Soft shackles: Lighter and safer than metal D-rings. They won't become projectiles if they fail.
  • Working Load Limit (WLL): Your gear should be rated for at least 2x your vehicle's weight. 3x is better.
  • Our pick: Yankum Ropes — made in USA, lifetime warranty, trusted by the off-road community.

💡 General Tips

  • Buy once, cry once. Cheap accessories fail faster and cost more in the long run. Invest in quality for things that protect your truck or your safety.
  • Vehicle-specific > universal. This applies to seat covers, floor mats, tonneau covers — anything that needs to fit.
  • Read the 1-star reviews. They tell you more than the 5-star ones. Look for patterns in complaints.
  • Check return policies. Fit issues happen. Buy from places that accept returns without restocking fees.
